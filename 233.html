In the south of Ngari Prefecture, there is Kangrinboqe, a sacred mountain recognized by the four major religions, Lake Manasarovar, the head of the sacred Tibetan lake, the 100-kilometer Zanda Earth Forest National Geological Park and the 700-year-old Tsaparang.   Kangrinboqe, meaning "Mountain of the Gods" in Tibetan, is the sacred spokesperson for Ngari Prefecture. It is also considered the centre of the world by Hinduism, Tibetan Buddhism, the native Tibetan religion of Bon, and ancient Jainism. Kangrinboqe, the main peak of the Gangtis Mountains, is only 6,656 meters above sea level, but its mountain form is unparalleled in its magic and natural beauty. Kangrinboqe is a stable and sacred pagoda in every sense of the word, and the summit is covered with snow all year round. A huge trough of ice running vertically down the summit and a horizontal layer of rock form the "swastika", the Buddhist symbol of spiritual power and eternal dharma.

  Reaching JI WU Temple at about 5:30, we have a panoramic view of Lake Manasarovar.

  Lake Manasarovar, Tibetan language meaning "eternal undefeated jasper lake". Climbing over, Lake Manasarovar shape like a full moon, the lake blue, blue waves lightly swing, white clouds and snow peaks reflecting which, the scenery is beautiful. Lake Manasarovar may be due to the stunning scenery, the proud geographical location, the spirituality of the dense flora and fauna, so it was given with all sorts of mysterious religious meanings, many religious books and legends have recorded and described Lake Manasarovar. Many religious texts and legends have described Lake Manasarovar. In the Buddhist classics, a lake was once called "the mother of the world's rivers", which means Lake Manasarovar.

  The lake is teeming with waterfowl, while the mosquitoes are so big and plentiful that it feels like they could carry people away!

Ngari Prefecture Northern Route

  Compared to the southern route, the northern route of Ngari Prefecture is more of an uninhabited wilderness. Desolate, vast, expansive, primitive, wild, and "wrong again and again". Often you can't see a single person for hours, but often you can see different kinds of wild animals on both sides of the so-called highway, most of which are Tibetan antelope, Tibetan donkeys and black-necked cranes. From the bottom of my heart, I don't want to have a highway to go through the wilderness, to keep the wilderness wild, or to be the right choice for mankind.

  Pangong Tso is an international lake, with about two-thirds of its length and area in Ngari Prefecture in Tibet, China, and the remaining third in Kashmir. It is interesting to note that although the lake belongs to the same family, the one in China is fresh water and produces the endemic Tibetan splittail, while the one in India is brackish, bitter and undrinkable, with no fish growing in it.
